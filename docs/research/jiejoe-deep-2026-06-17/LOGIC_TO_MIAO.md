# JieJoe Logic To Miao

## What The Site Actually Does

JieJoe's homepage is not a resume page. It behaves like a personal stage:

- A strong loading/identity moment establishes a graphic world before any content.
- Navigation is minimal. Contact stays fixed and obvious.
- Sections are not equal cards. Each major idea becomes a scene.
- Big type is used as environment, not only as a heading.
- The skills section uses role statements and playful artifacts instead of long explanations.
- The works section has a moving video strip, then a numbered list.
- The photos section uses a large outlined word behind overlapping cards and a direct invitation.
- The page asks the visitor to scroll, hover, and click instead of reading a conventional portfolio argument.

## What To Borrow

- Personal-site voice first, resume proof second.
- Fewer explanatory sections.
- A fixed social/contact layer.
- Work entry as a visual object: giant type + overlapping cards + clear enter/click line.
- Strong black/white/accent palette, but adapted to Miao's acid/chrome visual identity.
- Keep motion meaningful: entrance, focus, channel travel.

## What Not To Copy

- Do not copy JieJoe's name, illustrations, photo assets, or exact green/black composition.
- Do not use "photos of JieJoe" logic literally.
- Do not overfill with role labels if the user's work should feel more cinematic and AI-native.

## Required Redesign Move

The current page should stop presenting every chapter as a similar two-column block.

For Miao:

1. Hero introduces Miao as a person.
2. Works appears early as an interactive object.
3. Product/lab stays private and public-facing, with no internal IDs.
4. About/contact is simple and human.

The works section should use:

- Big outlined `WORKS` type behind the artwork.
- A fanned stack of original work images.
- Click/hover to pick a work.
- A TimeChannel-inspired 3D channel below the entry, using the user's original images as textures.
